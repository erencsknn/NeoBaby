The weight, height and head circumference of babies between the ages of 0-1 are important measurements that change rapidly. During this period, babies experience a rapid growth and development process. In the first months, babies gain weight quickly, and their weight usually doubles their birth weight. During the first year, babies' weight gain usually increases greatly, and this growth rate gradually slows down.

Average height reflects the growth process of babies' height. In the first months, babies grow rapidly and increase from about 50 cm to 60 cm at birth. During the first year, while babies increase in size, their growth rate slows down.

Head circumference is a measurement that reflects the brain development of babies. After birth, head circumference grows rapidly and increases significantly within the first year. The brain grows rapidly at an early age, and head circumference is used as an indicator of brain growth.
